WOT is Generative Design?
Generative art is a form of artistic endeavour in which the artist develops a process—be it a computer programme, a machine, a set of rules for natural language, or another procedural invention—and then sets it into action with a certain amount of autonomy to either contribute to or produce a finished work of art.

Offline Algorithmic Thinking
In this week's workshop, we learned about generative art: creating art with a set of rules, which leads to how we must make some kind of algorithm. To further explore this idea, we were told to create formulas with the throw of a dice. We must draw an object on the canvas, using pen and grid paper, but the qualities of the object: like its colors, size, and shape must be determined within the throw of a dice. In a pair of two with Ileana, we set on a formula to determine all qualities of the object, using just two dices (6-faced dice as Y & 8-sided dice as X). Within one throw, we could determine the object's colour (in RGB values), size, and the shape (using numbers on 6-faced dice: 1 = circle, 2 = ellipse, 3 = square, 4 = rectangle, 5 = triangle, 6 = circle).

Our formula works! But the distance within shapes is too small, so the spread of the shapes was unbalanced. This activity really got my brain going as I have to establish a mathematical formula. However, it is really stimulating to think what it would be like, if our life depended on the throw of a dice....

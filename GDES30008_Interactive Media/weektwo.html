WOT is the WWW?
Imagine a vast, interconnected universe where information flows freely across the globe at the click of a button - that's the World Wide Web. It's like a digital superhighway where websites are the destinations and hyperlinks are the roads that connect them. The Web isn't just about static pages; it's a dynamic, ever-expanding network where you can explore, connect, learn, and create. But what makes the Web truly remarkable is its accessibility. Anyone with an internet connection and a device can tap into this virtual realm, transcending geographical boundaries and bringing people together from all corners of the earth. It's a platform for sharing ideas, sparking conversations, and building communities. Hence, the World Wide Web is a gateway to an infinite universe of possibilities.

Hunt'n'Gather #2!
In this second Hunt'n'Gather, we were told to search for quirky webpages or web-apps. I found 3 webpages which I found really intriguing, the first is called Nomadic Tribe. This is an animated webpage, almost like a game where you can interact with the elements provided in the website. It tells the story of a group of people who have to move to a newfound island, leaving their old habitat. Users can click and hold to grow a plant, and this really inspired me for the base of my workbook website. The amazing features and interactivity of this site made me want to do the same with mine. What Nomadic Tribe really about is a new years greeting website, indicating that we should leave our bad old habits and move into fresh good ones for the upcoming year. The second page is from Nike, where we can customise our own 3D simulation of how the new Nike shoes feel like. The last webpage is a fun idea of "waking" up a sleeping site within 60 seconds. I feel like the interactivities of these two sites were compelling, enabling users to contribute and customise their own experience of the site.

For the Look! section, I looked at two websites, JODI.org and theuselessweb.com. These sites were very thought-provoking, especially the JODI one as I did not know exactly what it does, but it literally looks like a code presented in a webpage. It seems like a game but then I have to download it, which I did not click as it looks suspicious .__. Later I found out that the JODI site takes you to different interfaces everytime you click it, which actually, is really cool.
Paper Prototype (Crazy 8s)
During the studio class, we embarked on a thrilling journey of creativity through a brainstorming activity called Crazy 8s. It's like a whirlwind of ideas, pushing us to generate eight distinct concepts for how we could approach our website designs. With the clock ticking and pens flying, we feverishly sketched eight different home page layouts, each bursting with potential. This rapid-fire exercise ignited my imagination like never before, forcing me to think outside the box and explore unconventional ideas. Amidst the chaos, one concept emerged as a beacon of inspiration: the image of raining clouds nurturing a tiny seed, gradually blossoming into a magnificent flower.
It was a moment of pure clarity as I realized this concept perfectly encapsulated the essence of my web workbook - a journey of growth and transformation.
With newfound excitement, I'm ready to cultivate this vision into a mesmerizing digital experience that will captivate and inspire visitors.

External Research: Excursion!
Sometimes, stepping away from digital screens and pens is just what a creative soul needs. So, I decided to take a refreshing break and embarked on an excursion to the Queen Victoria Market. Amidst the vibrant chaos of the market, I explored each and every flower shop to find inspiration and references for my flower model. Eventually, I settled on a flower: the Orchid Cactus. Often overlooked for its unconventional appearance, this humble plant transforms into a breathtaking spectacle when it blooms. Its dinner-plate-sized flowers, adorned with silky petals in a myriad of hues, captured my imagination and sparked a wave of inspiration for my web workbook. This excursion was more than just a journey through the market; it was also a healing experience, reconnecting me with the simple joy and beauty of nature.

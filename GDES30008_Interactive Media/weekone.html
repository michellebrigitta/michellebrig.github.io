Hunt'n'Gather #1!
In the first Hunt'n'Gather, we were told to search for noteworthy interactivities and to watch coming-of-age movies. For human-computer interactivity, I found my research in something I have experienced on my own, The Crawling Man in NGV. Upon seeing this artwork, it really left an impression on me as it is very unsettlingly interesting. You can see dozens of box stacked up with a virtual reality of the crawling man. Additionally, you can also scan a barcode and therefore, the crawling man will be with you wherever you go. The digital screen is actually programmed to crawl for the length of the artist's life (36 years). The other two, Apple Vision Pro and Ray Ban Meta Glasses were something I am really interested in and makes me question what will the future society look like. For the movies, Iron Man is one of my all time favorite movie and so I was really happy to watch it again. I have always noticed how advanced the technologies were, but I never wandered how they actually work, so I thought it was really enticing to think about.
WOT is Interactive Media?
The first week begins by understanding the term "Interactive Media". Interactive media represents a computational approach influenced by cybernetics, autopoiesis, and system theories, challenging traditional understandings of reasoning, cognition, perception, memory, emotions, and affection. It encompasses various concepts such as interaction design, new media, interactivity, human-computer interaction, cyberculture, and digital culture, while also potentially incorporating augmented reality and virtual reality. The term "interactive media" encompasses any medium where there is an interface between the user/audience and the content, extending beyond electronic or digital formats to include analog forms like board games, pop-up books, flip books, and constellation wheels.

Human Computer in your Face!
In this workshop, we were given pieces of paper to write an activity we did that day. Then, randomly each person will pick the paper to recreate the gesture of the activity, without actually using the items. Most people wrote activities that they do with their phones, like swiping or scrolling on their phones, which made it really easy to guess. However, some people also wrote interesting ones like unlocking a car or locking the door. This was the highlight of week one!

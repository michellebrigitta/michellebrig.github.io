Refine for Submission
In the final moments before submission, I refined my website with little details to elevate the user experience and showcase a little bit of my personality. I incorporated a unique custom cursor that matches the concept of my site, which is a watering can. The cursor also changes into a water droplet for clickable buttons (Week One, Two, Three pages). To complete the concept of "growth", I introduced a captivating rain animation, imbuing it with dynamic movement and depth. Additionally, I included a favicon as taught in tutorials, a small yet impactful detail that enhances brand recognition and usability.

Hunt'n'Gather #3!
For the third Hunt'n'Gather, we gathered 3 engaging web archives or galleries of work. I found a lot of fantastic websites which I am fond of: Heilig Objects, Puppy Love, and Felix Hieronimus. All these websites show images or works in a refreshing way, making the user want to stay longer and see more works. The similarities of these websites are how they use animation and customization on their sites: color-changing header by scrolling, customizable background color, and changing shapes within clicks. On the Look! section, I looked at commercial, games, and museum websites to inspect the elements used: fonts, color palette, buttons, and features.
Fluid Design
In the workshops, we engaged in an insightful activity of "Fluid Design," aimed at mastering the art of responsive web design. The task is to create a design that seamlessly adapts to various screen sizes, primarily mobile. As I brainstormed and sketched a wireframe of my web workbook's mobile layout, I quickly realized the intricacies involved in ensuring optimal usability across different devices. With each element meticulously adjusted in size, I gained a deeper appreciation for the importance of responsive design. This activity served me a valuable lesson, highlighting the need to prioritize adaptability in today's digital landscape.
Redesigning the Scenery
Feeling dissatisfied with the simplicity of the scenery design in my workbook from the previous week, I embarked on a journey of creative transformation. This process began with an exploration of inspirations, seeking out visual cues that would inject depth and richness into my work. After settling on a soft color palette that resonated with my vision, I sketched the outlines of the illustrations. Then, utilizing Adobe Illustrator, I filled in the sketches with gradient colors, layering depth and texture to give life into the scene. This resulted in a depiction of harmonious mountains and lush meadows, a scene that truly speaks to my aesthetic sensibilities. This evolution in my scenery design not only reflects my growth as a designer, but also accentuates how prominent experimentation and iteration is in a world of design.
